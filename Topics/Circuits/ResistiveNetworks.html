Resistive, Inductive, and Capacitive Networks (Series, Parallel, and Equivalent Circuits)

Overview

Resistive, inductive, and capacitive networks form the backbone of circuit analysis. Simplifying series and parallel combinations of resistors, inductors, and capacitors allows for efficient calculation of voltages and currents in complex circuits.

Series Circuits

  • Definition: Components are connected end-to-end, forming a single path for current.
  • Total Resistance: The equivalent resistance is the sum of individual resistances:

    \[ R_{\text{eq}} = R_1 + R_2 + \ldots + R_n \]

  • Total Inductance: The equivalent inductance is the sum of individual inductances:

    \[ L_{\text{eq}} = L_1 + L_2 + \ldots + L_n \]

  • Total Capacitance: The equivalent capacitance is calculated as:

    \[ \frac{1}{C_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{C_1} + \frac{1}{C_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{C_n} \]

Parallel Circuits

  • Definition: Components are connected across the same two nodes, forming multiple paths for current.
  • Total Resistance: The equivalent resistance is calculated as:

    \[ \frac{1}{R_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{R_n} \]

  • Total Inductance: The equivalent inductance is calculated as:

    \[ \frac{1}{L_{\text{eq}}} = \frac{1}{L_1} + \frac{1}{L_2} + \ldots + \frac{1}{L_n} \]

  • Total Capacitance: The equivalent capacitance is the sum of individual capacitances:

    \[ C_{\text{eq}} = C_1 + C_2 + \ldots + C_n \]

Equivalent Impedance in Mixed Circuits

  • Concept: Mixed circuits consist of series and parallel combinations of resistors, inductors, and capacitors.
  • Simplification:
    • Reduce complex networks step-by-step by identifying and simplifying series and parallel combinations.
    • Continue until the circuit is reduced to a single equivalent impedance.

Understanding series and parallel resistive, inductive, and capacitive networks is critical for analyzing more advanced circuits, including those with dependent sources, power calculations, and transient responses.
