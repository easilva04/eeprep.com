Complex Numbers

Core Concepts

  1. Definition:
    1. A complex number is expressed as: \[ z = a + jb \]
    2. where:
      • \(a\) is the real part
      • \(b\) is the imaginary part
      • \(j\) is the imaginary unit where \(j^2 = -1\)
  2. Polar Form:
    1. Complex numbers can be written in polar form: \[ z = r\angle\theta = r(\cos\theta + j\sin\theta) = re^{j\theta} \]
    2. where:
      • \(r = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\) (magnitude)
      • \(\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{b}{a})\) (phase angle)
    3. The polar form is particularly useful in multiplying and dividing complex numbers. For example, the product of two complex numbers in polar form is given by: \[ z_1 \cdot z_2 = r_1 r_2 \angle (\theta_1 + \theta_2) \]

Applications in Engineering

  1. AC Circuit Analysis: Complex numbers are used to represent impedances and phasors in AC circuits. The impedance of a circuit element can be expressed as a complex number, and phasor analysis simplifies the calculation of voltages and currents.
  2. Signal Processing: Complex numbers are used in the analysis and design of filters and communication systems. The Fourier transform, which converts a time-domain signal into its frequency components, uses complex numbers.
  3. Control Systems: Complex numbers are used to analyze the stability and response of control systems. The poles and zeros of a transfer function are represented as complex numbers in the s-plane.
