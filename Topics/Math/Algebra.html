Algebra

Core Concepts

  1. Linear Equations:
    1. Solving single-variable equations: \(ax + b = 0\)
    2. Systems of linear equations: Using methods such as substitution, elimination, and matrix operations.
    3. Slope-Intercept Form: \( y = mx + b \)
    4. Point-Slope Form: \( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \)
    5. Standard Form: \( Ax + By = C \)
    6. Graphing linear equations and understanding intercepts.
    7. Applications in circuit analysis and signal processing.
    8. Linear inequalities and their solutions.
    9. Applications in optimization problems in engineering.
    10. Graphing linear inequalities.
    11. Understanding and solving absolute value equations.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Linear Equations

  2. Quadratic Equations:
    1. Standard form: \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)
    2. Solution techniques: Factoring, completing the square, and the quadratic formula.
    3. Factored Form: \( a(x - r_1)(x - r_2) = 0 \)
    4. Vertex Form: \( y = a(x - h)^2 + k \)
    5. Quadratic Formula: \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)
    6. Graphing quadratic functions and understanding parabolas.
    7. Applications in control systems and signal processing.
    8. Complex roots and their interpretation.
    9. Applications in analyzing resonance in circuits.
    10. Solving quadratic inequalities.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Quadratic Equations

  3. Inequalities:
    1. Solving linear and quadratic inequalities.
    2. Graphical representation of solutions.
    3. Linear: \( ax + b > c \) or \( ax + b < c \)
    4. Absolute Value Inequalities: \( |x| < a \rightarrow -a < x < a \)
    5. Absolute Value Inequalities: \( |x| > a \rightarrow x < -a \text{ or } x > a \)
    6. Compound inequalities and their solutions.
    7. Applications in optimization problems in engineering.
    8. Quadratic inequalities and their solutions.
    9. Applications in engineering and physics.
    10. Graphing inequalities in two variables.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Inequalities

  4. Polynomials:
    1. Operations: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
    2. Factoring techniques and theorems.
    3. Sum of Cubes: \( a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2) \)
    4. Difference of Cubes: \( a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2) \)
    5. Difference of Squares: \( a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b) \)
    6. Graphing polynomial functions and understanding their behavior.
    7. Applications in signal processing and control systems.
    8. Polynomial long division and synthetic division.
    9. Rational root theorem and its applications.
    10. Graphing higher-degree polynomials and understanding end behavior.
    11. Finding zeros of polynomial functions.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Polynomials

  5. Exponential and Logarithmic Functions:
    1. Understanding exponential growth and decay.
    2. Solving exponential and logarithmic equations.
    3. Applications in population dynamics and finance.
    4. Properties of logarithms and their applications.
    5. Graphing exponential and logarithmic functions.
    6. Applications in signal processing and control systems.
    7. Natural logarithms and their properties.
    8. Change of base formula.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

  6. Trigonometry:
    1. Understanding angles and their measures.
    2. Trigonometric functions and their graphs.
    3. Solving trigonometric equations.
    4. Applications in physics and engineering.
    5. Unit circle and radian measure.
    6. Trigonometric identities and their proofs.
    7. Inverse trigonometric functions and their applications.
    8. Law of Sines and Law of Cosines.
    9. Applications in navigation, physics, and engineering.
    10. Graphing trigonometric functions.
    11. Amplitude, period, and phase shift.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Trigonometry

  7. Precalculus:
    1. Understanding functions and their properties.
    2. Graphing various types of functions.
    3. Limits and continuity.
    4. Introduction to calculus concepts.
    5. Sequences and series, including arithmetic and geometric sequences.
    6. Binomial theorem and its applications.
    7. Parametric equations and polar coordinates.
    8. Vectors and their applications in physics and engineering.
    9. Conic sections: Parabolas, ellipses, and hyperbolas.
    10. Complex numbers and their properties.

    Resources: Khan Academy: Precalculus

Applications in Engineering

  1. Control Systems: Designing and analyzing system stability and response.
  2. Signal Processing: Analyzing and manipulating signals using algebraic techniques.
  3. Structural Analysis: Using algebraic techniques to analyze forces and moments in structures.
  4. Electrical Circuits: Applying algebra to solve circuit equations and analyze circuit behavior.
